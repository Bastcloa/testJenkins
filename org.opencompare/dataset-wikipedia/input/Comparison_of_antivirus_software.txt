This is a non-exhaustive list of ''notable'' [[antivirus software|antivirus]] and [[Internet Security]] [[software]], in the form of comparison tables, according to their platform (e.g. [[Desktop computer|desktop]] and [[Server (computing)|server]], [[Mobile device|mobile]], etc.) and their [[operating system]]s (e.g. [[Microsoft Windows]], [[Apple OS X]], [[Linux]], [[Oracle Solaris]], [[Google Android]], [[Apple iOS]], [[Ubuntu Touch]], [[Windows Phone]], etc.).

== Legend ==
The term "on-demand scan" refers to the possibility of performing a manual scan (by the user) on the entire computer/device, while "on-access scan" refers to the ability of a product to automatically scan every file at its creation and/or subsequent modification.

The term "[[Cloud computing|CloudAV]]" refers to the ability of a product to automatically perform scans on the cloud.

The term "[[Email security#Privacy concerns|Email Security]]" refers to the protection of emails from [[Computer virus|viruses]] and [[malware]], while "[[Antispam|AntiSpam]]" refers to the protection from [[Spam (electronic)|spam]], [[Confidence trick|scam]] and [[phishing]] attacks.

The term "[[Web filter|Web protection]]" usually includes protection from: infected and malicious [[URL]]s, [[phishing]] websites, online identity protection (privacy) and online banking protection.

Many antivirus products use "third-party antivirus engine", this means that the antivirus engine is made by another producer, however the malware signature and/or other parts of the product may (or may not) be done from the owner of the product itself.
